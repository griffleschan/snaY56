Class Relationship Survey for Y6 - Term 1

1. The graphs are dynamic! You can zoom in and zoom out, and pull apart the nodes using your mouse.
2. An arrow pointing from Student A to Student B means that Student A has identified Student B as a friend.
3. Bidirectional arrows means that they have chosen each other as friends.
4. Number in brackets next to each student's name and size of node indicate the number of classmates who have chosen this student as a friend (the higher the number, the higher his/her popularity). You may want to take note of loners too (those who have all arrows pointing out and no arrows pointing in, or they have not identified any friends, or chose not to do the survey).
5. Students who did not take the survey and those who did not indicate any friends in the class are also included.
